About me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of Illinois, Urbana‑Champaign. Prior to joining UIUC, I received a Master's in Mathematics, Statistics, and Economics from the African School of Economics.

My interests broadly lie in development and labor economics. In my job market paper, I study the implications of job protection and rule‑based tenure reforms for employee retention and student learning in Peru.

I originally hail from Benin, grew up in Cotonou, and have also lived in Lomé, Accra, and Ouagadougou. I am on the 2024–2025 Job Market.
