Welcome

I'm Wilfried Adohinzin, a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of Illinois, Urbana‑Champaign. My research focuses on development and labor economics, particularly how job security, tenure reforms, and institutional rules shape teacher retention, performance, and student learning.

In my job market paper, I study the impacts of teacher tenure reforms in Peru that link permanent contracts to performance on exams. I show that job security improves teacher retention—especially for high‑quality teachers—and raises student learning, though effects are more limited for disadvantaged students.

My broader work explores immigration enforcement and housing markets, the long‑run labor market effects of childbirth, and how environmental shocks such as temperature affect classroom dynamics. I am passionate about policies that promote human capital and equal opportunities.
